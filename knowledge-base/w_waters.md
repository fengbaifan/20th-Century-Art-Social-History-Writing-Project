# William George Waters

**1844**–**1928** | United Kingdom 英国

## 传记 / Biography

Private scholar, biographer and historian of Italian Renaissance art.&nbsp;Waters was the son of William Waters whose family traced its Norfolk, lineage back to Elizabeth I.&nbsp;He was educated at Ipswich Grammar School and Worcester College, Oxford where he won student&nbsp;awards for his historical scholarship.&nbsp; Independently wealthy, he settled in London writing, traveling and entertaining.&nbsp; He married Charlotte Leeder (d. 1868).&nbsp;After her death, in 1880 he married a second time to Emily Paton, an author and collector of cook books. He actively translated historic important scholarly texts beginning with&nbsp;Salernitano&nbsp;Masuccio's stories from the fifteenth century in 1895.&nbsp;In 1901 he published a small volume in Bell's art book series, Great masters in painting and sculpture on Piero della Francesca.&nbsp;Afterward he advised another amateur scholar, Evelyn Sandberg, for her book on the same subject and may have recommended the neophyte author&nbsp;to her&nbsp;Italian publisher. He died at age 83.
